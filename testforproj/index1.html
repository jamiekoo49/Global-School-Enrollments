Global School Enrollment and Its Impacts

Sahana Dhar, Madelyn Redick, Sophia Watanabe, Jamie Koo

Introduction

Global school enrollment is a critical indicator of educational access and quality worldwide, impacting individual and societal development in numerous ways. This topic is important and interesting for several reasons. Firstly, higher enrollment rates are often associated with a more educated workforce, which can drive innovation, enhance productivity, and contribute to economic growth. Analyzing global school enrollment data can provide insights into future economic prospects for different countries and regions. Secondly, education is linked to a wide range of societal and health benefits, including mortality rates. Exploring the impacts of school enrollment can highlight the importance of education in achieving broader societal goals. Lastly, data on global school enrollment can inform policymakers, international organizations, and investors about areas where educational infrastructure and support are most needed. This can help direct resources more effectively, ensuring that investments in education yield the greatest societal and economic returns.

Our dataset on global education is sourced from The World Bank, and specifically focuses on school enrollment. The dataset is a comprehensive collection of data that spans multiple countries and covers various years. With 43,629 rows and 67 columns of data, this dataset offers an in-depth look at educational indicators, including enrollment rates across different education levels (primary, secondary, and tertiary) and other relevant metrics such as progression to secondary education by gender. This dataset enables a wide range of analyses from trend analysis and comparative studies across regions or countries to evaluate the impact of educational policies. Furthermore, it allows for exploring the connection between education and factors like economic growth, health outcomes, and gender equality
